Tell your non-tech friend a story about a time you:
(1) Got blocked on a simple problem
What was the problem?

I had to write a simple function ( a bit of code ) that changed the background colour.

What problem solving techniques did you use?

I tried going through the code and then reading the error message. There wasn't one! That's when i realised I had to call the function or invoke it for it to work!!

How did you feel throughout the process?

I felt curious as to why it wasn't working. It wasn't particularly complicated and similar to an example so I was a little worried that maybe I had underestimated the problem!

What did you learn?

It's best not to panic, look at the obvious problems such as spelling and missed brackets, etc. Looking at the error message is very good for this.

Tell your non-tech friend a story about a time you:
(2) Solved a problem in an elegant way
What was the problem?

I had to do a coding exercise using where I had to check for certain condtions and then give different outputs under different condtions for all the answers to the problem.

What problem solving techniques did you use?

I used Pseudocode to clarify how I would tackle the problem.

How did you feel throughout the process?

I felt good that I was thinking logically about the problem and I was fairly confident that my logic was good. I just had to make sure my syntax was correct.

What did you learn?

It is good to think about how you want to solve a problem. Pseudocoding is very helpful in these cases to work out your logic. In simple cases its good to try something in slightly more complicated cases it helps to pseudocode.
